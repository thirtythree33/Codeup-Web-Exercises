The Essence of Finance

Goal of finance: using financial information, tools, and models, search for and implement "optimal" enterprise solutions that balance expected value with expected risk.

Expected future cash flows.

Risk: Possible deviations from those expected cash flows.

Financial Statements

Summaries of a business’ financial position at a point in time or business’ activities over a period of time. The overriding criterion by which accounting choices should be judged is decision usefulness. To be useful, information should possess the following qualitative characteristics:


  1. Relevance - Accounting information is relevant if it makes a difference in a decision.
    1. Relevant information helps users forecast future events (predictive value), or it confirms or corrects prior expectations (feedback value).
    2. Information must be available to decision makers before it loses its capacity to influence their decisions (timeliness).
  2. Reliability - Reliability of information means that the information is free of error and bias; it can be depended upon (note the inherent conflict between relevance and timeliness versus reliability)
    1. First readability section
    2. To be reliable, accounting information must be (verifiable) – we must be able to prove that it is free of error and bias (neutrality).
    3. The information must be a (faithful representation) of what it purports to be – it must be factual.
  3. Comparability and consistency
    1. Comparability means that the information should be comparable with accounting information about other enterprises.
    2. Consistency means that the same accounting principles and methods should be used from year to year within a company.

Balance Sheets

Measure of financial position at a point in time, showing what the firm owns (assets), and who has claims on what the firm owns (liabilities and owners’ equity).

Elements of the Balance Sheet

  1. Assets
    1. Current Assets
      1. Cash and short-term financial instruments
      2. Accounts Receivable
      3. Inventories
      4. Prepaid expenses
    2. Noncurrent Assets
      1. Longer term financial instruments
      2. Operating Assets
      3. Intangible Assets
  2. Liabilities
    1. Current Liabilities
      1. Accounts Payable or Trade Payable
      2. Debt Payments owed within one year
      3. Accrued Expenses
    2. Noncurrent Liabilities
      1. Long-Term Debt
      2. Capital Leases
      3. Defined benefit plan liabilities
      4. Deferred Taxes
  3. Owners' Equity
    1. Contributed Capital
    2. Retained Earnings
